About the Mayer Multiple

The Mayer Multiple is a financial metric used to analyze the current price of Bitcoin and other altcoins relative to their long-term moving average. It is calculated by dividing the current price of the coin by its 200-day moving average.

How to interpret

  1. Current Mayer Multiple: This number shows the ratio of the current price to the 200-day moving average. A value greater than 1 indicates that the price is currently above the historical average, suggesting bullish conditions, while a value less than 1 suggests bearish conditions.
  2. Historical Trends: The dashboard displays historical data on the Mayer Multiple. Look for patterns in how the Mayer Multiple correlates with price peaks and troughs to gauge market sentiment over time.
  3. Comparative Analysis: Compare the Mayer Multiples across different cryptocurrencies. Coins with a higher Mayer Multiple might be experiencing relatively stronger upward price momentum compared to those with a lower multiple.
  4. Actionable Insights: Use the Mayer Multiple in conjunction with other indicators to make informed investment decisions. A high Mayer Multiple could suggest a potential sell signal if historically it aligns with overbought conditions, while a low Mayer Multiple might indicate a buying opportunity if it aligns with historical market bottoms.

Approach

  1. Get prices - Fetch daily prices from the CoinGecko API with JavaScript
  2. Calculate Mayer Multiple - Mayer Multiple = Current Price / 200-day moving average
  3. Line Plot - Plot the Mayer Multiple of the selected asset
