Understanding Beta in the Altcoin Market

If you're familiar with finance concepts, you've likely encountered the term "beta." As defined by Investopedia, beta (β) measures the volatility—or systematic risk—of a security or a portfolio in comparison to the market as a whole, typically benchmarked against the S&P 500. Securities with betas higher than 1.0 are considered more volatile than the market.

In this project, I explore the concept of beta within the altcoin sphere, aiming to determine how the volatility of an altcoin compares to that of Bitcoin, which serves as the crypto world's benchmark.

Measuring Altcoin Volatility

The beta coefficient gauges the volatility of an individual altcoin relative to the more established cryptocurrency, Bitcoin. Statistically, beta represents the slope of the regression line through various data points. In the crypto context, each data point represents the return of an altcoin against Bitcoin's returns. This helps us understand the correlation between an altcoin’s movements and those of Bitcoin.

Types of Beta Values for Altcoins

Let’s consider a scenario where Bitcoin has a daily return of +10%:

  • Beta Value Equal to 1.0: If an altcoin like Ethereum or Solana shows a beta of 1.0 against Bitcoin, it indicates a strong correlation with Bitcoin's price movements. For a Bitcoin return of +10%, such an altcoin would also likely see a +10% return.
  • Beta Value Less Than One: An altcoin with a beta less than 1.0 is considered less volatile compared to Bitcoin. This implies that any price change, positive or negative, will be less pronounced. In our example, this altcoin would expect a return of less than +10% (smaller magnitude but same direction).
  • Beta Value Greater Than One: An altcoin with a beta greater than 1.0 is more volatile relative to Bitcoin. For instance, if Solana has a beta of 1.2, it could be expected to increase by +12% in our scenario, reflecting 20% greater volatility than Bitcoin.
  • Negative Beta Value: Although less common, some altcoins may exhibit a negative beta relative to Bitcoin, indicating an inverse correlation. An altcoin with a beta of -1.0 would typically move in the opposite direction to Bitcoin, similar to financial instruments like put options or inverse ETFs in traditional markets.
